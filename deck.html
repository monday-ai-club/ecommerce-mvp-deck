A typical mid-market brand runs on 5–10 disconnected systems — Shopify, ad platforms, ERP, 3PL, spreadsheets. Each silo holds critical data. None of them talk to each other. No automated action is triggered across them. The team spends its time connecting dots that should connect themselves.

Risks and Mitigations

We see the risks.
Here is how we manage them.

⚠️
3rd-party platform API dependency
Mitigation: Platform-agnostic architecture with graceful degradation. Polling + webhook hybrid. Engineered around from day one.
⚠️
Low AI adoption among customers
Mitigation: Human-in-the-loop for all actions in MVP phase. Agent suggests, human approves. Trust is built incrementally before full autonomy is unlocked.
⚠️
monday not positioned as an eCommerce industry player
Mitigation: We start with customers who already use monday — brand trust is pre-established. GTM is expansion, not conquest.
⚠️
First monday product where monday isn't its own customer
Mitigation: Design partners replace internal dogfooding. Earlier and deeper partner engagement compensates. This is a known gap — not a surprise.

Pricing model — consumption-based
💳 Flat base fee — board access, dashboards, standard automations (~$500–$1,500/mo depending on tier)
⚡ AI operations credits — pay per agent action: detection run, risk score, resolution trigger. Volume discounts at scale.
📈 Consumption expands naturally — high-GMV brands run more ops, pay more. Land-and-expand without sales pressure.
📊 Methodology: Mid-market brand (10K SKUs, 24/7 monitoring) ≈ 720 detection runs/day × 30 days = ~21,600 actions/mo. At $0.35/action = ~$7,560/mo base. Validates ~$8K/mo target.

The specific problem we're solving
Mid-market eCommerce brands lose revenue daily when inventory runs out while ad spend keeps running — campaign and inventory data live in separate silos, and nobody catches the mismatch in time. The MVP proves an AI agent can detect this, score the revenue at risk, and trigger the right action — automatically.
