The Problem

eCommerce teams lose revenue because critical issues are detected too late

Real scenario

A hero SKU trends to stockout before a peak weekend. Campaigns keep driving traffic. The team notices Saturday morning. Result: lost revenue, wasted spend, and a reactive scramble — every single week.

Industry data: stockouts cost retailers ~$1T in lost sales annually (IHL Group). For Shopify mid-market brands, a single peak weekend stockout erases 15–25% of weekly revenue.
Why it keeps happening
  • Shopify storefront performance lives in one system
  • Meta Ads performance lives in another
  • Inventory and fulfillment signals live in a third
  • Teams manually connect the dots under time pressure — costly issues are detected late, acted on slowly
Why This Matters

This is a high-frequency, high-cost problem

📦
Lost sales from stockouts — avg. 8–15% of monthly GMV for mid-market brands during peak periods
💸
Wasted ad spend pushing traffic to out-of-stock products — up to 30% of campaign budget lost during peak events
⏱
Delayed detection — teams typically notice issues 18–36 hours after they start, compounding damage
🔥
Reactive coordination that drains focus — avg. 4+ hours per incident of manual, cross-team triage
Operators don't need another place to look.
They need a system that tells them what matters, why, and who should fix it — before the damage is done.
